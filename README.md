# I-En Lee FAPs Master Thesis
# Viscosity-controlled syringe pump for precise filling of a urologicalimplant with high-viscosity oils

Stress urinary incontinence is much more than a personal affliction; it is a silentepidemic with profound effects on the quality of life of millions of people and aconsiderable burden on healthcare systems. To address the shortcomings of currenttherapies, research is being conducted into an intraurethral implant for the treatmentof urinary incontinence, which offers a minimally invasive, intuitive treatment solutionthat can also be used by women thanks to a completely new approach.\n\n
The aim of the work is to develop a viscosity-controlled syringe pump for filling thecentral component of the implant with high-viscosity oil (up to 60.000 mPas).The following tasks must be completed as part of the work:\n
。Development of a syringe pump for BBraun Inkjet Luer Lock Solo 2 ml syringeswith a focus on an efficient workflow for filling the implant. Result: CAD assemblyof the system\n
。Creation of control and operating software based on the Arduino Giga DisplayShield with input parameters (fill quantity in uL, viscosity in mPas) and outputparameters (duration of the extrusion process). Result: UI, parametercalculation, and motor control\n
。Construction and validation of the system in the laboratory based on a QMsystem according to ISO 13485 (provided by the institute). Result: Functioningand validated syringe pump for implant filling\n\n
